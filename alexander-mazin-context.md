## The ICP - Who This Is For

**Primary buyer:** Danish clinic owner, physiotherapy or adjacent specialties (acupuncture, chiropractic, manual therapy, pain specialist). Revenue 50K-500K DKK/month. Solo or small team (1-3 reception staff or no dedicated receptionist at all).

**The emotional driver:** "Ro i maven" (peace of mind). This owner is answering evening calls on their personal phone. They are not looking for technology - they are looking for relief from the phone. The AI Receptionist is not a software product in their mind; it is the thing that lets them close their laptop at 5pm.

**What they are NOT:** They are not tech-forward. They are not running Zapier workflows. They may use EasyPractice because it is the dominant Danish clinic management tool, but they chose it because it was recommended, not because they evaluated it. They are not going to self-configure Twilio. That is exactly why the White-Glove Install bump converts at a projected 55%.

**What they fear:**
- That the AI will answer in bad Danish or sound robotic to patients
- That it will book appointments into the wrong slots
- That they will not be able to set it up themselves
- That it will break something with their existing EasyPractice workflow

Each of these fears has a direct counter in the offer design (Danish voice training, EasyPractice pre-built integration, DIY video library, White-Glove Install bump).

**What they want to hear:** One Danish clinic owner, telling another, that they stopped answering evening calls and it worked. That is the core webinar proof structure.

## The Offer Ladder - Five Tiers With Full Reasoning

### Front-End: AI Receptionist Lifetime Access + 3 Months of Calls - $997

**The core offer:** Lifetime platform access (never expires) + 3 months of calls included to get started. When the initial call pack runs out, top up at launch-member rates. The platform is owned forever; the calls are metered fuel.

**Why $997:** Just above the psychological "big purchase" threshold but still within what a clinic owner can approve without a committee. US competitors charge $3,600-6,000/year. $997 once is self-evidently better math. The workshop attendees who paid to attend have already self-qualified at higher price points.

**The guarantee:** "Install AI Receptionist and use it for 30 days. If it doesn't free up at least 5 hours a week of phone time, email us and we'll refund every penny. You keep the prompt library and Skool access." Conditional guarantee lowers risk without opening refund abuse.

**Bonuses stacked around the core:**
- Danish-Language AI Voice (trained on clinic vocabulary, not generic Danish)
- EasyPractice + EasyMe pre-built integration (no manual calendar setup)
- DIY Install Video Library (2-3 videos Alex records)
- Skool Community Access (other clinic owners sharing workflows and wins)
- Prompt Library for Clinic Scenarios (appointments, cancellations, referrals, FAQs)

Total stack value: $2,500+. Today: $997.

### Order Bump: White-Glove Install - $297

**What it is:** A 1:1 install session with Alex's tech team (30-60 minutes). Technician handles Twilio setup, EasyPractice and EasyMe integration, Danish voice calibration, and runs 3 test calls live. The buyer goes from "paid and nervous" to "live and working" in 60 minutes.

**Why 55% projected take rate:** Clinic owners are not technical. $297 is less than one hour of front-desk salary at Danish rates. The bump removes ALL risk of the "I bought it but never set it up" outcome. For a $997 buyer, $297 is a rounding error if it guarantees the product actually works.

**Positioning:** "We'll come to you. We'll set it up. We'll run 3 test calls together before we sign off." This is not software support - it is a done-with-you implementation guarantee.

### OTO1: Annual Pass + Reactivation Engine - $2,997

**The core offer:** 12 months of calls (vs 3 months in the front-end) + the Reactivation Engine feature unlocked.

**Why $2,997 after $997:** The buying motive shifts from "get started" (front-end) to "all-in for a year + a second revenue stream I had not planned on." The math works: one recovered lapsed patient pays for the upgrade.

**The Reactivation Engine - how it works:**
The buyer uploads their patient list from EasyPractice. The system identifies every patient who has not booked in 90+ days. It runs a 3-wave AI voice outreach sequence:
- Wave 1: "We noticed you haven't been in for a while. Ready to book?"
- Wave 2 (no response after 5 days): Follow-up with a seasonal hook or new service mention
- Wave 3 (no response after another 5 days): Final nudge with a soft urgency close

Projected math: 300 lapsed patients in a typical clinic x 10% response rate x 150 DKK average booking = 4,500+ DKK recovered in the first 30 days from a feature the buyer did not know they were buying.

**Why the Reactivation Engine is ONLY at U1, not in the front-end:** It is the scarcity and reward mechanic for committing to the annual tier. If it were in the front-end, U1 has nothing distinctive to sell. It is the right feature at the right tier: it takes 12 months of calls to run meaningful reactivation campaigns across all their lapsed patients, so the annual commitment and the reactivation feature are naturally paired.

**Deliverables:** Upsell 1 landing page, VSL script, Reactivation Engine feature spec, 3-wave Danish voice script templates.

### Downsell: 6-Month Pass - $1,497

**Who sees this:** Buyers who declined U1 ($2,997 annual).

**What it is:** 6 months of calls at launch pricing. No Reactivation Engine (that stays at U1). Lower commitment, lower price.

**Why not include the Reactivation Engine here:** If the Downsell includes the same feature at $1,497 that U1 offers at $2,997, U1 has no reason to exist. The Reactivation Engine must stay above the Downsell price point to protect the U1 take rate.

**Positioning:** "Not ready for the full year? Get 6 months at launch pricing before this window closes." Simple and clean.

### OTO2: AI Advisory - $7,999 (OPTIONAL - Alex's Call)

**What it is:** Monthly advisory program. Group-only (not 1:1). Includes: monthly outside expert workshops (practitioners who have scaled clinics), daily async feedback channel in Skool, weekly group coaching calls with Alex. **The $830/month installment alternative:** $7,999 upfront OR $830/month x 12 months. The installment option widens the applicant pool to clinic owners who would not write a $8,000 check in one go but can absorb $830/month as a business expense.

**Why this is NOT the same product as Go-To Klinikken Mastermind:** The Mastermind includes 1:1 access, clinic visits, retreats, and a very selective founder community. **Protecting existing Mastermind members:** Do not discount Go-To Klinikken. Do not create a separate SKU for mastermind members. Do not cross-reference the products. The Advisory is a separate product, positioned separately, with a genuine operational difference that justifies the price gap.

## The "Calls Not Credits" Rule

**External language:** Always say "calls." Clinic owners understand calls. "3 months of calls," "annual pass," "6 months of calls." Never say "credits," "tokens," "usage," or "minutes" in any customer-facing copy.

**Internal language:** Credits, usage, Twilio minutes, metered consumption - whatever is accurate for the engineering and billing side. Jordan and Alex can say "credits" internally. The product surface and all copy says "calls."

**Why this matters:** "Credits" sounds like a SaaS platform that will nickel-and-dime them. "Calls" sounds like a phone plan they already understand. The ICP uses landlines. They know what a call is.

## The Lifetime + Metered Fuel Model - The Honest Version

This model has to be communicated clearly, not buried in terms and conditions.

**What "lifetime" means:** The platform - the AI, the integrations, the account, the login - is theirs forever. They will never be billed a subscription to access the platform itself. This is a genuine lifetime license.

**What "metered" means:** Each call the AI answers consumes a small amount of credit (Twilio + AI inference cost). The 3-month starter pack covers a typical clinic's call volume for roughly 3 months. When the pack runs out, the buyer tops up at "launch-member rates" - a permanent discount locked in at purchase, never available to later buyers.

**Why we rejected burying this in the T&Cs:** Alex's Trustpilot moat is 4.9/5. That score will collapse if the first 20 buyers feel deceived. Danish consumer protection law is also strict on material omissions. The right move is to explain the model clearly in the offer (platform is lifetime, calls are like a phone plan you top up) and lean into the launch-member pricing as a feature, not a disclaimer.

**How to frame it on the page:** "Think of it like owning a phone. The phone is yours forever. Calls cost what calls cost. We've locked in your call rate for life - you'll always pay what you paid today, never more."

## Reactivation Engine Logic (Deeper Cut)

The Reactivation Engine is the most compelling monetization argument in the entire launch because it generates revenue for the clinic owner, not just saves them time.

**Full mechanics:**
1. Buyer exports their patient database from EasyPractice as a CSV (standard feature, takes 2 minutes)
2. They upload the CSV to the AI Receptionist dashboard
3. The system filters for patients with no booking in the last 90 days
4. It generates a 3-wave outreach sequence in Danish, personalized with the patient's name and last treatment type
5. Wave 1 goes out on Day 1. Wave 2 goes out on Day 6 if no booking. Wave 3 goes out on Day 11 if still no booking.
6. Every patient who books creates a calendar event in EasyPractice automatically.

**The financial argument for U1:** A typical Danish physio or acupuncture clinic has 200-400 active patients. After 12 months of running a practice, they have 300-600 lapsed patients (people who had one or two appointments and never rebooked). At 10% reactivation rate and 1,000 DKK per session, the first reactivation campaign can generate 30,000-60,000 DKK in revenue from patients they had written off. One campaign pays for U1 multiple times over.

**Why this is only at U1 (not front-end):** Clinics need a full year of calls to run proper reactivation campaigns. You cannot reactivate 300 lapsed patients on 3 months of front-end calls without burning through the pack. The annual pass is the correct pairing. The constraint is real, not manufactured.

**Call volume estimate:** A small Danish clinic answers 5-20 calls/day. 90 days of front-end calls at 10 calls/day = 900 calls. A reactivation campaign for 300 lapsed patients could require 900 outbound AI calls before replies. This is why the front-end call pack is intentionally not sized for reactivation - it requires the annual upgrade.

## Day of Event Sequence - 3 Emails (Launch Day)

**Segmentation rule:** Tag live attendees vs non-attendees. Live attendees get Email 1 during the event. Non-attendees skip Email 1 and go straight to Email 3. Do not tell a non-attendee about a live-only bonus they were never eligible for.
