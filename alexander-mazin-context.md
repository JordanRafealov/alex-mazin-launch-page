## The ICP - Who This Is For

**Primary buyer:** Danish clinic owner, physiotherapy or adjacent specialties (acupuncture, chiropractic, manual therapy, pain specialist). Revenue 50K-500K DKK/month. Solo or small team (1-3 reception staff or no dedicated receptionist at all).

**The emotional driver:** "Ro i maven" (peace of mind). This owner is answering evening calls on their personal phone. They are not looking for technology - they are looking for relief from the phone. The AI Receptionist is not a software product in their mind; it is the thing that lets them close their laptop at 5pm.

**Cash reality:** This ICP is cash-constrained. They run profitable clinics but don't have $3,000 sitting in checking for a single capital purchase. They're used to monthly SaaS subscriptions (EasyPractice, Xero, booking tools) and approve them without committee when they're under ~$300/month. Above ~$550/month becomes a "let me think about it" conversation. All installment pricing is engineered around these thresholds.

**What they are NOT:** They are not tech-forward. They are not running Zapier workflows. They may use EasyPractice because it is the dominant Danish clinic management tool, but they chose it because it was recommended, not because they evaluated it. They are not going to self-configure Twilio. That is exactly why the White-Glove Install bump converts at a projected 55%.

**What they fear:**
- That the AI will answer in bad Danish or sound robotic to patients
- That it will book appointments into the wrong slots
- That they will not be able to set it up themselves
- That it will break something with their existing EasyPractice workflow

Each of these fears has a direct counter in the offer design (Danish voice training, EasyPractice pre-built integration, DIY video library, White-Glove Install bump).

**What they want to hear:** One Danish clinic owner, telling another, that they stopped answering evening calls and it worked. That is the core webinar proof structure.

## The Offer Ladder - Four Tiers With Full Reasoning

### Front-End: AI Receptionist Lifetime Access + 3 Months of Calls - $997

**The core offer:** Lifetime platform access (never expires) + 3 months of calls included to get started. When the initial call pack runs out, top up at launch-member rates. The platform is owned forever; the calls are metered fuel.

**Why $997:** Just above the psychological "big purchase" threshold but still within what a clinic owner can approve without a committee. US competitors charge $3,600-6,000/year. $997 once is self-evidently better math. The workshop attendees who paid to attend have already self-qualified at higher price points.

**The guarantee:** "Install AI Receptionist and use it for 30 days. If it doesn't free up at least 5 hours a week of phone time, email us and we'll refund every penny. You keep the prompt library and Skool access." Conditional guarantee lowers risk without opening refund abuse.

**Bonuses stacked around the core:**
- Danish-Language AI Voice (trained on clinic vocabulary, not generic Danish)
- EasyPractice + EasyMe pre-built integration (no manual calendar setup)
- DIY Install Video Library (2-3 videos Alex records)
- Skool Community Access (other clinic owners sharing workflows and wins)
- Prompt Library for Clinic Scenarios (appointments, cancellations, referrals, FAQs)

Total stack value: $2,500+. Today: $997.

### Order Bump: White-Glove Install - $297

**What it is:** A 1:1 install session with Alex's tech team (30-60 minutes). Technician handles Twilio setup, EasyPractice and EasyMe integration, Danish voice calibration, and runs 3 test calls live. The buyer goes from "paid and nervous" to "live and working" in 60 minutes.

**Why 55% projected take rate:** Clinic owners are not technical. $297 is less than one hour of front-desk salary at Danish rates. The bump removes ALL risk of the "I bought it but never set it up" outcome. For a $997 buyer, $297 is a rounding error if it guarantees the product actually works.

**Positioning:** "We'll come to you. We'll set it up. We'll run 3 test calls together before we sign off." This is not software support - it is a done-with-you implementation guarantee.

### Upsell 1: AI Clinic in a Box - $2,997 PIF or 6× $547/mo

**The core offer:** 9 AI engines running the entire clinic (acquisition + retention + operations) plus 6 months of AI Fuel that powers every engine including the receptionist itself.

**The 3×3×3 structure:** 3 categories, 3 engines each, mapping to how a clinic actually runs.

**Patient Acquisition (3 engines):**
1. **AI Review Engine** — automatically asks patients for Trustpilot/Google reviews after every appointment. Grows the public reputation that drives new patient inquiries.
2. **AI Referral Engine** — post-treatment, happy patients receive an automated ask to refer friends and family. Compounds the clinic's referral loop without staff involvement.
3. **AI Lead Capture** — website inquiries are captured by an AI agent that qualifies the lead and books them directly into EasyPractice. No lead falls through the cracks.

**Patient Retention (3 engines):**
4. **AI Reactivation Engine** — identifies patients who haven't booked in 90+ days and runs a 3-wave Danish voice outreach sequence to bring them back. One campaign typically recovers 30,000-60,000 DKK from patients the clinic had written off.
5. **AI No-Show Recovery** — catches late cancellations, immediately reaches out to the waitlist and recent-inquiry list, and rebooks the slot the same day. Stops empty chairs from costing revenue.
6. **AI Follow-Up Flow** — post-treatment check-ins and treatment plan adherence nudges. Patients who stay compliant book more appointments; AI does the follow-up staff never have time for.

**Clinic Operations (3 engines):**
7. **AI Intake Assistant** — new patient forms, pre-appointment prep, triage into the right EasyPractice category. Cuts staff admin time to near-zero per new patient.
8. **AI FAQ Concierge** — website chatbot in Danish, available 24/7, handles the 80% of repeat questions (hours, parking, pricing, specialties) that drown the phone line.
9. **AI Operations Assistant** — internal tool for Alex's team. Daily call summaries, staff comms, calendar intelligence, and flagging anomalies (e.g., unusual drop in bookings).

**Plus 6 months of AI Fuel:** Powers all 9 engines for 6 months at typical clinic usage levels. Combined with the Front-End's 3 months, the buyer is covered for 9 months total. At month 9, they renew at launch-member rates — permanently locked at today's pricing.

**Why $2,997 (3× the Front-End):** Clean anchor psychology — 3x jump from FE signals a meaningful upgrade without price-shocking the clinic owner. Under the threshold where the buyer starts shopping around.

**Why 6× $547/mo as the installment option:**
- $547/month sits intentionally below the $550 psychological barrier where clinic owners move from "fits our software budget" to "committee decision."
- 6-month window matches Alex's preferred collection cadence — faster cash, shorter default exposure than 12-month plans.
- 19% installment premium over PIF ($3,282 vs $2,997) — market-normal, not punitive. The broke audience that chooses installments doesn't feel cheated.
- **Elegant symmetry:** the 6-month installment window matches the 6-month fuel allocation, so the buyer's fuel runs out exactly as their installments end. Built-in month 9 renewal moment → baked-in year 2 revenue.

**Why PIF at $2,997 saves $285:** Small enough discount to protect installment revenue, meaningful enough to pull some buyers to PIF. Expect 20% PIF take (broke audience) / 80% installment.

**The installment structure explained cleanly:**
- Two options only — PIF or 6-month installments. No price menu, no slider.
- On checkout: "Pay in Full $2,997 (save $285)" and "6 payments of $547/month."
- Cleaner choice architecture than multiple installment lengths.

## The "Calls Not Credits" Rule — Updated

**External language for the Receptionist:** Always say "calls." Clinic owners understand calls. "3 months of calls." Never say "credits," "tokens," "usage," or "minutes" in customer-facing copy about the receptionist.

**External language for the 9 AI engines:** Call it "AI Fuel." The engines use a mix of calls, SMS, and AI inference — "fuel" is the umbrella metaphor that covers all three without violating the "calls" rule. Sticky, intuitive, clinic-owner-friendly. "6 months of AI Fuel powers all 9 engines."

**Internal language:** Credits, usage, Twilio minutes, metered consumption, AI inference cost — whatever is accurate for engineering and billing. Jordan and Alex can say "credits" internally. The product surface and all copy says "calls" (for the receptionist) and "AI Fuel" (for the engines).

**Why this matters:** "Credits" sounds like a SaaS platform that will nickel-and-dime them. "Calls" and "Fuel" sound like things they already understand. The ICP uses landlines. They know what a call is. They know what fuel is.

## The Lifetime + Metered Fuel Model - The Honest Version

This model has to be communicated clearly, not buried in terms and conditions.

**What "lifetime" means:** The platform - the AI, the integrations, the account, the login, every engine they've purchased access to - is theirs forever. They will never be billed a subscription to access the platform itself.

**What "metered" means:** Each call the AI answers + each AI engine interaction consumes a small amount of fuel (Twilio + AI inference cost). The 3-month starter pack covers a typical clinic's receptionist volume. The 6-month AI Fuel in U1 or Downsell covers typical usage for however many engines that tier includes. When fuel runs out, the buyer tops up at "launch-member rates" - a permanent discount locked in at purchase, never available to later buyers.

**Why we rejected burying this in the T&Cs:** Alex's Trustpilot moat is 4.9/5. That score will collapse if the first 20 buyers feel deceived. Danish consumer protection law is also strict on material omissions. The right move is to explain the model clearly in the offer and lean into the launch-member pricing as a feature, not a disclaimer.

**How to frame it on the page:** "Think of it like owning a phone and a car. The phone is yours forever. The calls cost what calls cost. The AI engines run on fuel. We've locked in your rate for life — you'll always pay what you paid today, never more."

**Fair-use language in T&Cs:** Each tier's fuel allocation is "sized for a typical clinic workload — up to X calls + Y SMS + unlimited AI inference per month. Heavy users top up at launch-member rates." Caps set generously enough that 95%+ of clinics never hit them, tight enough that a rogue heavy-user doesn't bankrupt unit economics. Alex's dev team to set exact caps after reviewing beta clinic usage data.

## Reactivation Engine Logic (Deeper Cut)

The Reactivation Engine is the most compelling monetization argument in the entire launch because it generates revenue for the clinic owner, not just saves them time. It lives in U1 (as one of 9 engines) and in the Downsell Revenue Trio (as one of 3 engines).

**Critical:** It's an engine the buyer installs and runs themselves, not a service Alex's team delivers. Zero ongoing service labor. Pure software shipping.

**Full mechanics:**
1. Buyer exports their patient database from EasyPractice as a CSV (standard feature, takes 2 minutes)
2. They upload the CSV to the AI Receptionist dashboard
3. The system filters for patients with no booking in the last 90 days
4. It generates a 3-wave outreach sequence in Danish, personalized with the patient's name and last treatment type
5. Wave 1 goes out on Day 1. Wave 2 goes out on Day 6 if no booking. Wave 3 goes out on Day 11 if still no booking.
6. Every patient who books creates a calendar event in EasyPractice automatically.

**The financial argument for U1:** A typical Danish physio or acupuncture clinic has 200-400 active patients. After 12 months of running a practice, they have 300-600 lapsed patients. At 10% reactivation rate and 1,000 DKK per session, the first reactivation campaign can generate 30,000-60,000 DKK in revenue from patients they had written off. One campaign pays for U1 multiple times over.

**Call volume estimate:** A small Danish clinic answers 5-20 calls/day. A reactivation campaign for 300 lapsed patients could require 900 outbound AI calls before replies. The 6-month AI Fuel in U1 is sized to cover both inbound receptionist traffic + heavy outbound workflows like reactivation, with fair-use caps preventing extreme edge cases.
